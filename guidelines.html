Publishing Guidelines

Ship Clean. Ship Fast.

These guidelines help your submission get approved faster and keep the Loom registry safe for everyone. Uploads are moderated before they become public.

Checklist

Submission Basics

  • Clear name + short description (what it does, who it’s for).
  • Valid version string (recommend Semantic Versioning: 1.2.3).
  • At least one image preview that matches the asset.
Safety

No Malware

Do not upload executables or scripts designed to steal data, disrupt systems, or bypass security.

  • Keyloggers, stealers, ransomware, trojans.
  • Obfuscated payloads or “dropper” installers.
  • Anything that attempts to exploit or abuse users.
Moderation

Review Flow

Uploads start as Pending, then become Approved or Rejected.

If rejected, update your submission and try again—clear descriptions and accurate previews help.

Naming + Versioning

Be Predictable

Use consistent names and versions so installs and updates are reliable.

  • Asset name: descriptive and stable (avoid “NEW!!!”, emoji spam, or misleading names).
  • Version: recommend MAJOR.MINOR.PATCH (e.g., 2.1.0).
  • Changelog: summarize what changed and any breaking changes.
File Limits

Keep It Reasonable

Current limits are tuned for fast uploads and safe review.

  • Asset file size: up to ~100 MB.
  • Chunked uploads: files are split into smaller chunks for reliability.
  • Images: upload preview images that clearly represent the asset.
Tip: If your upload is rejected for size or content, trim unused files, compress media, and remove bundled third‑party content you don’t have rights to ship.
IP + Copyright

Upload Only What You Own

Don’t upload copyrighted content you don’t have permission to distribute. That includes paid assets, ripped media, or re-uploads of another creator’s work. If you’re publishing a fork or derivative, clearly attribute and follow the original license.
